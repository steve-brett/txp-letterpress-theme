I just joined Twitter so I can keep up with all the fun new things that people keep coming up with.

There seems to be a lot of cool AI tools being launched. I saw this one that removes backgrounds from images of people and gives you a PNG with transparency.

Well that's impressive and dang useful. https://t.co/99tspvk4lo Cool URL too.

(Remove Image Background 100% automatically – in 5 seconds – without a single click) pic.twitter.com/k9JTHK91ff

— CSS-Tricks (@css) December 17, 2018

I used a picture of myself up at the Clifton suspension bridge. It's from Facebook, so you can see the compression.

Here I am with my background removed! I floated the image and used the CSS shape-outside: url("image.png") to wrap this text around it.

As you can see, I still need to do some adjustment of the spacing. I can't seem to get it to work using padding. I'll have another go and update this article!

Look up shape-outside on MDN web docs.
